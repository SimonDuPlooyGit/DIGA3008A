    Reflections on an ethical internet

    Published by Simon Du Plooy on the 19th of May 2025

    I am racking my brain over how I imagine an ethical internet should look but I am really struggling to come up with a solid representation or idea. Other than the digital divide limiting people's access to the internet, or ad spots existing for certain sites to pushed, I feel that the internet is already a technology that is very open. I think the way that people used to think about the internet, and the way the internet functions, is already the most ideal circumstance to having an "ethical internet". Ideally it already exists as a space/technology where anyone can freely put information and content out into the world. It should and does have the capability to connect anyone with one another and to information. An "ethical internet" can only be achieved by an ethical society/humanity. I believe everything unethical that may be related to the internet is due to people and how, what and why they put content onto the internet. The internet is a just a reflection of what happens in the real world. I do think that what happens on the internet is a heightened form of what happens off the internet, but it starts from there nonetheless.
